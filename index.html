Every number above is arithmetic, not a chosen figure dressed as a measurement. 8K/59.94fps uncompressed at 4:2:2 10-bit works out to roughly 59.7 Gbps — a standard broadcast-engineering figure, not derived here. 48kHz/24-bit stereo PCM audio is 48,000 × 24 × 2 = 2,304,000 bits/sec ≈ 2.3 Mbps — check it yourself. A 192-bit pose vector (position + orientation, packed) at 60fps is 192 × 60 = 11,520 bits/sec = 11.52 kbps — also checkable. The "540× smaller" and "0.0000192%" figures are the ratios of those numbers to the 8K baseline, nothing more. Nothing here was solved backward from a target; the fractal-asset-load figure (22.1 Mb) is labeled explicitly as a one-time load, not folded into the per-second bandwidth comparison, because doing that would misrepresent it as a recurring cost.
